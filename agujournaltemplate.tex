\section{Science Goal 1: Inferring the 3D Plasma and Magnetic Structure of Heliospheric Drivers}
\begin{description}
\item[Champion profile] Solar, heliosphere, CME, or reconstruction person.
\item[Candidate section title] Inferring the 3D Plasma and Magnetic Structure of Heliospheric Drivers.
\item[Core science question] How can we infer the 3D plasma and magnetic structure of solar and heliospheric transients from sparse observations, and how does that structure evolve through the heliosphere?
\item[Board gaps] Line-of-sight projection effects in solar observations; unknown CME magnetic-field orientation and structure; sparse observations of heliospheric plasma; evolution of CMEs through the heliosphere; transport of magnetic field from photosphere to heliosphere; multi-point observations for turbulence and solar-wind structure.
\item[Main argument] Prediction fails early when the solar or heliospheric driver is not characterized in 3D. The key missing information is not only event arrival time, but magnetic topology, orientation, plasma distribution, and uncertainty.
\item[Concrete output] Gap table with columns: Gap, Missing observable, Consequence for prediction, Needed capability.
\item[Possible figure contribution] Schematic of projected solar observations leading to inferred 3D heliospheric structure and then to Earth or planetary impact.
\end{description}

\section{Science Goal 2: From Heliospheric Forcing to Coupled Geospace Response}
\begin{description}
\item[Champion profile] Magnetosphere, ionosphere, thermosphere, or radiation-belt person.
\item[Candidate section title] From Heliospheric Forcing to Coupled Geospace Response.
\item[Core science question] How do solar-wind and CME properties control energy partitioning, plasma transport, and response across Earth's magnetosphere-ionosphere-thermosphere system?
\item[Board gaps] Geomagnetic response to different CME configurations; energy deposition through solar-wind-magnetosphere coupling; partitioning between neutral heating, plasma transport, chemistry, and wave dynamics; radiation-belt response; high-energy ion confinement; magnetosphere-ionosphere-thermosphere coupling; realistic constraints in analytical solar wind-magnetosphere models.
\item[Main argument] Even if the heliospheric driver is known, prediction requires understanding how energy and momentum are partitioned across coupled geospace regions.
\item[Concrete output] Response-chain diagram linking solar-wind or ICME driver, magnetospheric coupling, ionospheric electrodynamics, thermospheric heating, radiation belts or plasma transport, and impacts.
\item[Slide mapping] Friday Slide 2 or 3: where the chain breaks at the heliosphere-Earth interface.
\end{description}

\section{Science Goal 3: Background State and Preconditioning Across Solar Timescales}
\begin{description}
\item[Champion profile] Upper atmosphere, solar minimum, solar cycle, or long-term variability person.
\item[Candidate section title] Background State and Preconditioning Across Solar Timescales.
\item[Core science question] How do solar-cycle and solar-minimum conditions shape the background state of Earth and planetary environments, and how does that background state modulate transient impacts?
\item[Board gaps] Solar-minimum and solar-cycle forcing; effects of variable solar conditions on Earth and planetary environments; background atmospheric and geospace state; why similar solar events produce different impacts; long-term versus transient forcing; climate and weather questions framed carefully through upper-atmosphere and geospace response.
\item[Main argument] Transient responses cannot be understood without the background state. Solar minimum, solar-cycle phase, and prior activity precondition the atmosphere, ionosphere, thermosphere, radiation belts, and magnetosphere.
\item[Concrete output] Timescale table with columns: Timescale, Solar forcing, Response domain, Key unknown, Needed observations.

\section{Science Goal 4: Universal and Planet-Specific Responses to Solar Forcing}
\begin{description}
\item[Champion profile] Planetary magnetosphere, Saturn, or comparative plasma environment person.
\item[Candidate section title] Universal and Planet-Specific Responses to Solar Forcing.
\item[Core science question] Which responses to solar forcing are universal across planetary environments, and which are controlled by planet-specific properties?
\item[Board gaps] Solar-wind interaction with planetary magnetic fields; effects of planetary mass, magnetic field, and plasma sources; Saturn and other internally driven magnetospheres; solar-wind properties across planetary distances; common metrics for comparing planetary response; habitability questions only if framed as environmental response, not broad exoplanet speculation.
\item[Main argument] Earth provides the richest data, but other planetary environments reveal which processes are general and which depend on intrinsic magnetic fields, rotation, plasma sources, atmospheric properties, and heliocentric distance.
\item[Concrete output] Comparative response table with columns: Environment, External driver, Internal state, Response metric, Transferable lesson.
\item[Important scope control] This section should stay concise. It should not become the Theme 3 Mars or habitability paper. Its purpose is to support the broader Sun-to-planet coupling argument.

\section{Community Recommendations and Near-Term Implementation}
\begin{description}
\item[Champion profile] Organizer or synthesis lead.
\item[Candidate section title] Community Recommendations and Near-Term Implementation.
\item[Purpose] Turn the science goals into actionable community steps.
\item[Recommendation 1] Develop benchmark events and intervals. Include both transient events and quiet-time / solar-minimum intervals.
\item[Recommendation 2] Improve 3D characterization of heliospheric drivers. Prioritize CME/ICME plasma and magnetic structure, uncertainty, and evolution.
\item[Recommendation 3] Connect heliospheric drivers to coupled geospace response. Develop response frameworks that link magnetosphere, ionosphere, thermosphere, radiation belts, and plasmasphere.
\item[Recommendation 4] Link background state and transient response. Treat solar-cycle phase, solar minimum, preconditioning, and recovery as essential parts of prediction.
\item[Recommendation 5] Create open, AI-ready, uncertainty-aware workflows. Use benchmark datasets, data assimilation, model intercomparison, and interpretable ML to support prediction.
