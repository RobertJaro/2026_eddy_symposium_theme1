\begin{abstract}
Solar variability affects Earth and planetary environments through a connected chain of processes linking solar drivers, heliospheric structure, and geospace or planetary response. This white paper defines a focused roadmap for turning solar influence on Earth and planetary environments into an integrated, predictive Sun-to-planet framework. It does not duplicate the Decadal Survey by setting broad mission or program priorities. Instead, it identifies interdisciplinary gaps, benchmark events and intervals, shared data products, modeling capabilities, and community actions that can connect solar drivers, heliospheric propagation, and environmental response \cite{placeholder}.

\begin{plainlanguagesummary}
Solar variability affects Earth and other planetary environments through both long-term changes and short-lived events. Prediction remains difficult because solar, heliospheric, and geospace observations and models are often developed separately. This paper identifies practical steps to connect these communities, including shared benchmark events, open data products, improved three-dimensional descriptions of solar drivers, coupled response models, and uncertainty-aware workflows.
\end{plainlanguagesummary}
\end{abstract}
